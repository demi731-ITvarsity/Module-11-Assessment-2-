Chinese Vegetable Spring Rolls

Fry It Up China

Preferred dipping sauces are different for the Shanghai and Cantonese clans as well. Shanghai spring rolls are always served with vinegar, but dim sum places serve theirs with whatever condiment you want. Some like it with plum sauce, hot oil, chili garlic sauce, vinegar, or just plain soy sauce. I think this spring roll recipe tastes good without any condiments, but I also like an old classic: a Worcestershire-based dipping sauce I used to have in New York’s Chinatown, where I had my first taste of dim sum spring rolls. It’s a little tangy and goes nicely with the fried spring rolls.
